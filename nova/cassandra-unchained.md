## Who is Michael Burry?

Dr. Michael J. Burry (MD, Vanderbilt) is a physician-turned-investor who founded Scion Asset
Management. He is best known for predicting and profiting from the 2008 housing collapse, as
documented in Michael Lewis's *The Big Short* (2010). He deregistered Scion as an investment
adviser in late 2025 and launched the Substack **Cassandra Unchained**
(michaeljburry.substack.com) on November 23–24, 2025.

The name is self-conscious: Cassandra was the mythological prophet whose true predictions were
fated to be disbelieved. Burry frames his own career this way — correct about the direction,
ignored until too late.

Subscription: $379/year. The newsletter gained 23,000+ subscribers within 24 hours of launch.

### 2. Dot-Com Bubble (draft line 128: "… Dr. Burry's ideas on the dot com bubble …")

**Status: Well-sourced through Cassandra Unchained (November 2025).**

Burry addressed the dot-com era directly in his inaugural Cassandra Unchained post, titled
**"The Cardinal Sign of a Bubble: Supply-Side Gluttony"** (November 2025). Key arguments:

- He challenges the common claim that today's AI boom is different from dot-com because today's
  tech giants are profitable. His counter: the 1999 Nasdaq *was also* driven by profitable large
  caps — "Microsoft, Intel, Dell, and Cisco" — not just money-losing startups.
- The structural problem then and now: *"catastrophically overbuilt supply and nowhere near
  enough demand."*
- The dot-com era didn't fail because the companies were unprofitable; it failed because
  infrastructure investment vastly exceeded what the market could absorb. He argues AI is
  repeating this exactly.
- His summary of today's AI situation: *"it's just not so different this time."*

*(Sources: economistwritingeveryday.com summary, November 25 2025 —
https://economistwritingeveryday.com/2025/11/25/michael-burrys-new-venture-is-substack-cassandra-unchained-set-free-to-prophesy-all-out-doom-on-ai-investing/;
Yahoo Finance —
https://finance.yahoo.com/news/big-short-investor-michael-burry-162334180.html)*

**B&C framework fit:** Burry's "supply-side gluttony" framing maps directly onto Goldfarb &
Kirsch's *Window of Bubble Opportunity*: the period between initial investment and resolution of
business model uncertainty, during which leverage and competition risk are systematically ignored.

### 4. AI Bubble (draft lines 140–141: "… applying the framework … Dr. Burry's ideas on AI …")

**Status: Richest and most specific area. Best material for the essay.**

Cassandra Unchained launched explicitly as a vehicle to argue the AI boom is a bubble. Key
material (sourced from indexed excerpts and news reporting):

**"Glorious folly":** Burry's overall characterisation of AI investment — massive infrastructure
spend creating oversupply that demand may never catch up to.

**The Nvidia = Cisco argument** (from "The Cardinal Sign of a Bubble: Supply-Side Gluttony"):
> "And once again there is a Cisco at the center of it all, with the picks and shovels for all
> and the expansive vision to go with it... Its name is Nvidia."

Cisco lost over 75% of its value in the dot-com crash (some sources say ~90%). Burry's short
position against Nvidia (via put options, disclosed in 13-F filings, 2023 onward, and now through
Cassandra Unchained) is the trade expression of this thesis.

**The five horsemen:** Burry identifies "the five public horsemen of today's AI boom — Microsoft,
Google, Meta, Amazon and Oracle" as analogous to the profitable-but-overextended large caps of
1999. He adds that AI startups like OpenAI are the loss-making dot-coms.

**The depreciation warning:** Burry warns that hyperscalers are understating depreciation on AI
hardware by **$176 billion between 2026 and 2028** — implying reported earnings significantly
overstate true profitability.

**Market positions (confirmed via reporting):**
- Short Nvidia (put options, disclosed in 13-F filings and Cassandra Unchained)
- Short Palantir
- Short Tesla (renewed 2025)
- Long energy sector (Valero Energy, up 200%+ since 2020)

## 5. General Philosophy

- **Value investor in the Graham/Klarman tradition.** Distrusts narrative-driven markets; tends
  to be short when stories are loudest.
- **"Index fund bubble" (2019):** In a widely cited Bloomberg interview (September 2019), Burry
  argued that passive index investing had distorted price discovery — capital flowing into index
  constituents regardless of individual merit. This is an extension of the B&C "naïf investor"
  argument: the naïfs are index investors who provide capital without information about valuations.
  *(Searchable: "Burry Bloomberg passive investing bubble 2019")*
- **The burryblog (late 1990s–early 2000s):** His early public writing, now offline, forms the
  documentary basis for Michael Lewis's *The Big Short* (2010). His dot-com-era scepticism of
  "eyeballs" and "revenue multiples" is documented there and in Lewis's reporting.
- **On the cost of being right too early:** His Tesla experience is the sharpest case study —
  correct about direction, ruinous in the short run due to volatility and short-squeeze risk.
  He has stated this plainly. It's also the practical implication of the B&C framework: knowing
  a bubble exists does not produce a profitable trade.
