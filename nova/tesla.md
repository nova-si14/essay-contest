## Narrative Shifts Since 2019

### 1. Electric Vehicle Mass Market (2019–2021)
- Model 3 proving mass-market viability; Gigafactory Nevada scaling
- Massive stock run-up 2020–2021 on EV demand thesis

### 2. Twitter/X Distraction (2022–2023)
- Apr 2022: Musk makes $44B offer for Twitter
- Oct 2022: Acquisition completed; Musk becomes CEO
- Widely cited as key driver of Tesla's -73.6% crash (CEO distraction, political backlash)
- One report estimates Musk's political positioning cost Tesla over 1 million EV sales (2025)
- Jul 2023: Twitter rebranded to X

### 3. FSD (Full Self-Driving) Narrative (ongoing)
- Musk has promised unsupervised FSD repeatedly; timeline has slipped every year
- As of 2026: labeled "supervised FSD"; 8+ billion cumulative miles driven
- Cybercab (robotaxi, ~$30,000) announced Oct 2024; timeline unclear
- See Wikipedia's *"List of predictions for autonomous Tesla vehicles by Elon Musk"*
  for detailed timeline of missed deadlines — relevant to endogeneity-of-uncertainty thesis

### 4. Optimus Humanoid Robot (2022–2026)
- Musk claims Optimus = "potentially 80% of Tesla's future value"
- Oct 2024: "We, Robot" event; some robots were teleoperated (not autonomous)
- Jan 2026: Mass production of Optimus Gen 3 announced
- Q4 2025 earnings: Musk acknowledged no Optimus units are doing "useful work"
- Rodney Brooks (iRobot cofounder): called Musk's vision "pure fantasy thinking" (2025)

### 5. Musk in Politics / DOGE (2025)
- Role in Trump administration; DOGE (Department of Government Efficiency) lead
- Polarizing effect: some customers boycotted Tesla; political narrative extended

## Legacy Automakers: EV Scorecard

| Company | Development | Status (2025–2026) |
|---------|------------|---------------------|
| Ford | F-150 Lightning | **Discontinued Dec 2025** — unprofitable; replaced by EREV format |
| GM | Ultium platform (Silverado EV, Sierra EV) | Credible range (~480 mi) but margins weak |
| VW | ID. series | Significant EV push; European market focus |
| BYD (China) | Multiple models | **Major wildcard**: state-supported, competitive pricing; authors never predicted |

**Assessment:** The authors' bear thesis that legacy automakers would enter the EV market and compress Tesla's margins has partially materialized — but the real competitive pressure came from Chinese manufacturers (BYD), which the authors did not anticipate. Ford's F-150 Lightning discontinuation shows EV margins remain very difficult for legacy players.

## Essay Relevance

### Did the authors' 2019 prediction come true?
**Mixed — mostly wrong on the stock, partially right on the thesis.**
- The stock is ~27x its 2019 price. "Coming down to earth" has not happened overall.
- There was a 73.6% crash (2021–2023) — so the authors were right that a major correction would come, but the stock recovered to new highs.
- Musk's subsequent narrative shifts (FSD → Robotaxi → AI → Optimus) have continuously
  extended the "window of uncertainty," exactly as Annex 1 of the draft predicts.
- The legacy automaker entry thesis was partially right (Ford, GM entered) but underwhelming;
  the real disruption came from Chinese EV makers, which the authors missed entirely.

### Connection to Annex 1 (Endogeneity of Uncertainty)
Tesla is the clearest case in the book of an entrepreneur actively *manufacturing* uncertainty.
Each new narrative (FSD deadlines, Gigafactory, Optimus, AI) extends the window of bubble
opportunity and delays the day of reckoning. The FSD prediction list on Wikipedia is a
remarkable primary source for this.
