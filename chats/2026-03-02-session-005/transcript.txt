  ---
  What works well

  The opening is genuinely excellent. The Clarkson quote is clever and the
  single-line follow-up ("Bubbles have NEVER bankrupted anyone. Suddenly
  crashing, THAT'S what gets you.") is punchy, memorable, and sets the voice
  perfectly ‚ÄĒ exactly the right register for ACX.

  The framework summary is well-executed. You don't just list the four factors;
  you synthesize them into a three-step narrative (lines 52‚Äď57) that makes the
  framework feel like a story, not a textbook checklist. The "Window of Bubble
  Opportunity" footnote is a nice touch.

  The historical case studies have good rhythm and real variety. The contrast
  structure ‚ÄĒ electricity/radio (big bubbles) vs. telephone/TV (not bubbles, and
   here's why) ‚ÄĒ is effective pedagogy. The Burry material on electricity is
  vivid and concrete.

  Annex 1 (Endogeneity) is the sharpest piece of original thinking in the essay.
   The observation that the framework treats uncertainty as exogenous but then
  the authors use it as endogenous when analyzing Tesla is a genuine
  intellectual catch ‚ÄĒ the kind of thing that makes an ACX review stand out from
   a book summary. The stardust notes file fleshes it out with promising
  follow-up questions: lenders as the hard constraint, the role of ultra-low
  interest rates, analyst forecasts as "soft deadlines," the narrative of "AI
  2027" as an accidental bubble-popper. That's rich material.

  Annex 2 (Human Imagination) is interesting but more sketch-like. The
  connection between imagination's limits and slow technology deployment is
  well-grounded in the book. The questions about superforecasting and AI
  imagination are intriguing but undeveloped.

  ---
  Areas to watch

  1. The placeholder sections are the essay's biggest vulnerability. The AI
  section ‚ÄĒ the most important one ‚ÄĒ currently ends with "Yep, it's a bubble."
  after three bullet-point placeholders. Given that the essay sets up this
  payoff from the beginning ("let's just bite the bullet and apply this
  framework to AI"), leaving it underdeveloped will be the first thing readers
  notice.
  2. Edison chronology still needs fixing (line 89): the merger was 1892, not
  caused by the Panic of 1893. Small but verifiable.
  3. Two typos: "proffessors" (line 147) and "Accoding" (line 159).
  4. The Guerron-Quintana sentence (lines 19‚Äď20) is now directionally correct
  but structurally awkward ‚ÄĒ two nested comparisons in one sentence. Something
  to revisit once the rest of the draft is more complete.
  5. Annex 1 vs. its stardust notes: the annex in the draft is well-written but
  stops before the most interesting questions (lenders as constraint, interest
  rates, crypto, analyst forecasts). The
  stardust/reflections-on-endogeneity-of-uncertainty.md file has ideas that
  aren't yet in the draft annex.
  6. Uneven depth across case studies ‚ÄĒ Electricity gets a lot of space, Cars
  and Telephone get 3 bullets each. Whether that's by design or drafting order
  is up to you, but it's noticeable.

  Bottom line: The bones are strong. The opening, the framework summary, and the
   endogeneity insight are the best parts. The main gap is the AI section, which
   is currently the essay's IOU to the reader. What are you thinking of tackling
   next?
