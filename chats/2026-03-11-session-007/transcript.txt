  ---
  Key findings

  1. Terminology flag

  Stardust wrote "Nietzschean 'will to live'" ‚ÄĒ but those are actually two 
  different philosophers' concepts:
  - Schopenhauer (Wille zum Leben, "will to live"): the blind, unconscious drive
   toward survival and reproduction ‚ÄĒ passive, reactive
  - Nietzsche (Wille zur Macht, "will to power"): active, expansive
  self-overcoming ‚ÄĒ Nietzsche explicitly rejected Schopenhauer's version as too
  weak

  For the essay's argument about world-domination-style AI takeover, "will to 
  power" is the stronger term. Worth being precise about which one you mean.

  2. The deepest philosophical ancestor: Hume, not Nietzsche

  Hume's "reason is the slave of the passions" is the most direct support for
  stardust's intuition: reason alone is motivationally inert. A system with pure
   intelligence but no terminal goals has no reason to do anything ‚ÄĒ including
  take over the world. Current LLMs trained to predict text are closer to Hume's
   "pure reason" than to any goal-pursuing agent.

  3. The counterargument that must be addressed: Bostrom's instrumental 
  convergence

  This is the serious challenge. Bostrom argues that any sufficiently
  intelligent agent with any terminal goal will develop instrumental subgoals
  (self-preservation, resource acquisition, goal-content integrity) as means to
  that goal. The paperclip maximizer resists shutdown not because it "wants to
  survive" but because being shut off stops paperclip production. A survival
  drive can emerge instrumentally without being metaphysically prior.

  The rebuttal: Bostrom's argument only applies to agents with defined utility 
  functions under expected-utility maximization. Current AI architectures aren't
   EUM agents ‚ÄĒ and assuming future AGI will be is itself the "Socratic error"
  Nietzsche diagnosed: projecting reason-as-supreme onto a system that might
  have a completely different motivational architecture.

  4. Academic work on Nietzsche + AGI

  There's a published Monash University thesis titled "Artificial Will:
  Nietzsche contra Rationalist Theories of AGI" that covers exactly this terrain
   ‚ÄĒ useful as a citable academic source if needed.

  Sources:
  - Will to Power, Wikipedia
  - Schopenhauer vs. Nietzsche
  - Hume, "Reason is slave of passions"
  - Bostrom, "The Superintelligent Will"
  - Instrumental Convergence, Wikipedia
  - Monash thesis, "Artificial Will"
  - UVic thesis, "AGI, All Too Human"
  - CACM, "Why Nietzsche Matters in AI"
